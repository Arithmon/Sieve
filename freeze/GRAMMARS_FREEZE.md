## 1. What a grammar is here (skeleton 2.2)

A grammar G = (A, O, R) is:
- **A**: a finite alphabet of atomic constants (leaves), each with a declared
  numerical value (exact integer, exact rational, or high-precision real);
- **O**: a finite set of operations with arities (binary: + - * / ^ ; unary:
  sqrt, inverse, square, log where defined);
- **R**: well-formedness rules (domain guards: no division by zero, no log of
  non-positive, no 0^0; power exponents restricted to avoid trivial encodings,
  see section 4).

E(G, k) = all well-formed expressions over G of complexity at most k (measure in
section 3). The size |E(G, k)| as a function of k is figure F2 of the paper and
the denominator of every trials-factor computation.

## 2. The three grammars

### G_INT: small-integer arithmetic (baseline / null of nulls)
- A = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9} (integers 1..9)
- O = {+, -, *, /, ^, sqrt, inv, sq}
- Rationale: the weakest defensible alphabet. Any claimed structure must beat
  what bare small-number arithmetic already achieves. This grammar is also where
  inverse-symbolic-calculator folklore lives (RIES-style hits).

### G_STRUCT: declared structural alphabet (the contested one)
- A = generated by an explicit **closure rule**, not a hand-picked list.
  Rule SETTLED 2026-06-12 (D9, Brieuc): the union of
  - dimensions and ranks of all compact simple Lie groups of rank <= 8
    (A_n, B_n, C_n, D_n series members up to rank 8, plus G2, F4, E6, E7, E8),
  - orders of small structures canonically attached to them (Weyl group orders
    optional: flagged, likely too rich),
  - Betti numbers b2, b3 of the manifold family declared by the framework under
    test (for the GIFT case study: 21 and 77), plus the small integers 1..9.
- O = same as G_INT.
- Design principle: the rule must be statable in one sentence a hostile referee
  would accept as "what a motivated theorist could reach for". The alphabet is
  then whatever the rule generates; the framework under test does not get to
  prune it. Note the asymmetry: GIFT's 20 internal constants are a *subset* of
  this alphabet; the null gets the full set, which makes the null *stronger*
  (more ways to match by accident), hence conservative.

### G_TRANS: transcendental extension
- A = A(G_INT) plus {pi, e, phi (golden ratio), gamma (Euler), ln 2,
  zeta(3), zeta(5), zeta(7), zeta(9), zeta(11)}
- O = G_INT ops plus {log}
- Rationale: where "zeta values in physics" claims live (Axis 5 gate). Also the
  grammar in which historical cases (Eddington-style numerology with pi and e)
  are scored.

**Case-study note.** GIFT's claimed relations must be expressible in
G_STRUCT (rationals in topological integers) or G_TRANS (the few zeta/phi
relations). Which relations land in which grammar is recorded in the scorecard,
per relation, before the search runs.

## 3. The two complexity measures

### kappa_MDL: description length (primary)
- cost(leaf) = log2 |A| bits; cost(op) = log2 |O| bits; kappa(expr) = sum over
  the expression tree. Grammar-relative by construction: the same formula is
  cheaper in a richer alphabet, which is exactly the trade-off the trials factor
  must price (richer alphabet = cheaper formulas but bigger E(G, k)).

### kappa_NODE: node count (secondary, robustness)
- kappa = number of nodes in the expression tree. Blunt, but immune to alphabet
  bookkeeping disputes. Results stable across both measures = robust claim;
  results that flip = reported as fragile (skeleton 6.2).

## 4. Anti-gaming guards (R rules, to finalize at freeze)

- Power exponents: integer exponents in [-4, 4] only; general real powers
  excluded except through declared unary ops. Guard against encoding arbitrary
  precision in exponents.
- No nested powers deeper than 2 (a^(b^c) excluded: cheap precision pump).
- Canonicalization before counting E(G, k): commutative reordering and constant
  folding collapse duplicates, so the trials factor is over *distinct values*,
  not syntax trees. (This is where a Lean-certified count, skeleton 7.3, would
  bite: the canonicalization itself is the auditable step.)
- Match window: an expression "matches" an observable at z if
  |expr - x_exp| <= z * sigma_exp, with z in {1, 2} reported and the claimed
  framework's own precision per relation scored at its actual z.
