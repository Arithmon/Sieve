## 1. Inclusion criteria (the load-bearing part)

**C1. Dimensionless.** Only quantities with no units under a declared convention.
Angles are admitted with the unit declared (degrees). Quantities that are
dimensionless only relative to an arbitrary human convention (e.g. h =
H0 / (100 km/s/Mpc)) are excluded; the 100 is a typography choice, not physics.

**C2. Measured.** A current experimental central value and uncertainty exist from
a frozen edition of a standard compilation: PDG (2024/2025 edition, declared),
NuFIT 6.1 (ordering and SK-atm convention declared), Planck 2018. One edition per
source, dated, no later substitution (a future data update is a new freeze
version with its own DOI; verdicts against the old freeze stand).

**C3. Category completeness.** Categories are admitted, not individual constants.
Once a category is in, every member that passes C1/C2/C4 is in. This is the
anti-cherry-picking rule (skeleton section 6.3).

**C4. Algebraic independence within category.** One representative set per
category: no entry may be an exact algebraic function of other entries. Where a
choice of basis exists (e.g. which two of the three Omega ratios, which
parametrization of CKM), the basis is the standard-compilation parametrization,
declared here, chosen before any search. Derived combinations (Koide ratio,
J_CP, sums) are excluded as redundant; a framework is free to *predict* them,
but they are scored through the independent set.

**C5. Scheme and scale declared.** Running quantities are taken at the
compilation's standard reference point (MS-bar; light quarks at 2 GeV;
m_c(m_c), m_b(m_b); alpha_s(M_Z); sin2_theta_W(M_Z) MS-bar). Scheme sensitivity
is a robustness item (skeleton 6.x), not a tuning knob.

**Note on the GIFT Type I list.** The existing internal list
(`canonical/data/observables.json`, 33 Type I entries) fails C4 in at least two
places: it carries six algebraically dependent Omega ratios, and the Koide ratio
alongside the two lepton ratios that determine it. The frozen list is therefore
NOT the GIFT list; the case study (skeleton 5.3) will map GIFT's claims onto
this independent basis. Finding this discrepancy is the methodology working as
intended.

## 2b. Discrepancies found during verification (case-study relevant)

The verification pass surfaced differences between primary sources and the
internal GIFT dataset (`canonical/data/observables.json`). These do NOT affect
the freeze (which follows the primary sources) but must be reconciled in the
internal dataset and checked against the FoP supplements:

1. **sin2_theta13 (PMNS):** internal 0.02224 vs NuFIT 6.1 w/o SK-atm 0.02249(57).
   Possible cause: internal value taken from the with-SK-atm column or from 6.0.
2. **sigma_8:** internal 0.807 vs Planck VI Table 2 baseline 0.8111(60).
   Possible cause: different column or a derived S_8 quantity.
3. **m_c/m_s:** internal 11.7 was scale-mismatched; the consistent
   m_c(m_c)/m_s(2 GeV) is 13.75(13) (internal B16 "ext" 13.6 was close).
4. **m_s/m_d:** PDG quotes only a 17-22 band; the evaluated PDG ratios are
   m_u/m_d and m_s/m_ud. The internal exact claim m_s/m_d = 20 must be scored
   through the independent basis (C4), not against a hand-picked ratio.
5. **CKM A:** internal 0.839(11) is the UTfit-prescription variant; the PDG
   main global fit gives 0.826(16). The freeze uses the main fit.
6. **m_W/m_Z:** internal 0.8813(2) vs derived 0.88136(15) (consistent, newer
   m_W world average).

## 3. Explicit exclusions (and why)

| Excluded | Reason |
|---|---|
| h (Hubble in 100 km/s/Mpc units) | C1: convention-dependent normalization |
| Q_Koide | C4: function of Cat B entries |
| Omega_DM/Omega_b, Omega_c/Omega_Lambda, Omega_Lambda/Omega_m, Omega_c/Omega_m, Omega_DE | C4: dependent on Cat G basis (flat LCDM) |
| lambda_H | C4: same information as the admitted m_H/v (decision journaled: m_H/v has no factor-2 convention ambiguity) |
| m_s/m_d | C4 + source discipline: PDG evaluates m_u/m_d and m_s/m_ud, and quotes only a 17-22 band for m_s/m_d; the admitted pair determines it |
| J_CP (CKM or PMNS) | C4: function of angles + phase |
| m_tau/m_e | C4: product of Cat B entries |
| Individual masses, v_EW, M_W ... | C1: dimensionful |
| G_F-derived or scale-setting ratios | C1/C5: scale choice = framework degree of freedom |
